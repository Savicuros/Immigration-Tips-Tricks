Hermit Crab (lat. Paguroidea) is a quite fragile creature. Unlike his relatives - crabs and lobsters - he doesn’t have his own shell. Instead, he inhabits an empty gastropod shell in his surroundings to get protection. The shell will bring him safety, of course, but after some time it will prevent his further growth. As it becomes too small for his body, hermit crab has to move out, leaving safety to search for a new home.
